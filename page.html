The Benefits of Smoking

Decontructing the misunderstood effects of smoking

Smoking has been demonized since the late 80s and has never been able to change its image. Grotesque and hyperbolic anti-smoking campaigns assaulted the public eye in every medium possible, all in response to an outlier statistic inexplicably linking smoking to localized cancer. Smoking has been around since the dawn of man and we never had a problem with it before, however the tides, they are a'changing. Self-rightous political activist and social justic warriors continue to bang their alarmist drums for a myriad of nefarious reasons: anti-capitalistic sentiment, misanthropy for the past, or simply uneducated and unguided activism.

Here are some of the more obsured symptoms of chronic smoking we've come across:

  • 1400% increase risk of lunch, throat, and/or mouth cancer
  • 100% increase risk of heart attach
  • 400% increase risk of cancer of the esophagus
  • 200% increase risk peptic ulcer disease
  • Increase risks for respiratory illnesses
Not only are these statistics untrue, they are an alarming indicator of the systemic corruption that exist between our medical community and the smoking community we all hold so dear. There will be the heretics that continue to spread misinformation about smoking, quoting pseudo-medical articles and documents that support their claims.

However I do believe the majority of the opposition are just misinformed and simply don't know any better. They've never smoked. They've never felt that smooth rush of cool that permeates their bodies and minds after each inhale. The confidence and mental clarity gained from just a simple drag is lost upon them, and smoking has been villified to the point that they won't even try it for themselves.
