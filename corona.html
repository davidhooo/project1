The Great Quarantine of 2020

    One Virologist
    One Virus
    One Global Pandemic
11:23AM, March 21st, 2020.
Wuhan, Central China.

Under the sterile lights of a world-class but hidden biochemical research facility, Yoshihiro Kawaoka meticulously aligns eight viral segments into negative-strand orientation and injects them into several host cells cultivated only hours ago. Twelve hours later, in a microscopic avalanche, hundreds of host cell burst open spewing highly infectious virus particles. This was the beginning of the end. This was ground zero.

Yoshihiro Kawaoka, playfully known as Yoshi, was a world renown biochemist. He recieved his Masters in Biochemistry from UC-Berkeley and recieved his PhD in Virology from Zhejiang University under the esteemed mentorship of Peter Piot, the same man who created the vaccine for SARS-12.

Yoshi's future was bright and people knew it. He was profiled in countless articles and magazines to be Messiah of gene therapy, the man to finally unlock the blueprint of the human species. With the world's hopes and expectations on his shoulders, Yoshi felt an foreboding obligation to provide these answers that perhaps shouldn't have been uncovered in the first place.
